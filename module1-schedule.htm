Welcome

Trinity College Dublin provides a unique and vibrant learning experience and is proud to have produced generations of leaders, decision-makers, innovators and entrepreneurs. We welcome you to the Innovation Academy, an exciting intellectual space where industry leaders and educators interact to develop and nurture creativity and innovation.

 

Overview

This programme focuses on the continuing professional development of individuals who wish to return to the workplace after a career break due to caring obligations. It offers a unique combination of transferrable skills involving building confidence and overcoming psychological barriers to return to work in addition to gaining entrepreneurial skills required to start their own business.

 

How the Programme Works

The programme offers participants the opportunity to develop their creative potential by acquiring a range of problem solving and lateral thinking skills. Students experience and engage in the innovation process through interacting with entrepreneurs, business leaders and industry mentors in an informal learning environment.

 

Working within multidisciplinary teams, participants develop an entrepreneurial mind-set and a clear understanding of the societal, social and economic value associated with innovation. Students engage with industry projects hosted by private or public enterprise. Participants learn to communicate and interact with industry collaborators, and begin to appreciate the process involved in bringing an idea through to application. They develop an entrepreneurial mind-set, increasing their value to potential employers and/or carefully positioning them to plan a venture of their own.

 

Career Opportunities

This programme offers participants the opportunity to develop the personal qualities of responsibility, sociability, self-management and integrity. Specifically, participants develop the following soft skills essential to any employer:


- Communication: The ability to be articulate when speaking but also have the ability to listen.
- Leadership: Acquiring leadership and negotiation skills and be sensitive to cultural diversity. Learning to lead as well as follow and to work to tight deadlines.
- Adaptability: Being flexible enough to adapt and change in a dynamic work environment. Engaging problem solving abilities to tackle real world challenges.
- Teamwork: Working well in dynamic group settings and exercise cooperation and compromise.
- Integrity & Work Ethic: Maintaining a positive attitude and good work ethic.

 

 

On successful completion of this module, students should be able to:

  • Develop confidence to overcome psychological and practical barriers to returning to work.
  • Assess skills and abilities to identify relevant career pathways
  • Work effectively in as a team or individually within a creative context and a work environment.
  • Apply creative problem-solving skills to challenges and opportunities relating to industry project (Return ships) and career options
  • To communicate with and to interact with multiple stakeholders and establish professional networks.
